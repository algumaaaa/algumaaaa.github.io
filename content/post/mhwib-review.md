## One massive, breathtaking leap forward that didn't quite stick the landing.

This might be one of the most difficult games to rate of anything i've ever played. At times its a solid 4 with incredibly well done redesign of old monsters together with interesting new aditions to the cast. The quality of life features helping make the experience all the more smoother and the moment to moment gameplay feeling more polished than ever before. And then you're out of the hunt, and thrust into the tangled mess of a web that are the game's sorry excuse for monster hunter systems.

I don't care for the removal of the farm. I understand how repetitive it got but the solution to that i feel was in their hands already, you developed a whole house costumization system! how fun would it be to be able to design your farm to your heart's content like a miniature stardew you get to tinker with after every hunt? Instead we got a MENU for a farm, unless you also count iceborne's weirdest adition to the "pwease dont gwind in our gwinding game ;-;" mentality they sometimes adopt: The Steamworks! What were they thinking!!!!

I'm at the post-story part of iceborne right now so this next issue is greatly aliviated, but my god whenever you're reminded of the whole "everyone has to go in and watch a cutscene, then 3 people leave and join the 4th :DDD" side of this game it takes the air out of you with how absolutely stupid it is. Surely they knew of this, surely they understood how insane it is, i'm assuming executives ordered the expensive cutscenes to be mandatory so they can justify the expenses or something. Doesn't make it any less painful.

Don't get me wrong however. I wouldn't have played this game in any other way. Monster hunter was, is, and always will be a game to play with your friends. When I played my favorite MH game, Freedom Unite, I didn't have anyone to play it with, and the experience was definitively worse off for it. It was also fun meeting randoms online and just hunting random stuff with them. The community is generally a lot better in this game than the one I had the displeasure of experiencing in Freedom Unite. Anyway, thank you Rosa, Rude, and Suto for sticking with me throughout this 100 hour game, I'm sure my constant complaining must've gotten grading but they tanked it like it was nothing (:

### Me and my friends at journey's end.
![ib cleared](/iceborne-clear.png)

Performance is awful! You can really feel this being the deathrattle of the MT Framework engine. Its barely holding itself together as it conjures up 100+ threads of god only knows what the fuck its doing. Don't get me wrong, the game looks lovely! The hitboxes are great, the areas look fantastic, experiencing the visuals of this game has been an absolute treat. I just wish it ran on personal computers.

This next point is a very personal one and not one i expect people to agree with, but it ties into the previous one so here goes. The monsters look a bit too realistic for my taste. I often find myself feeling bad for what i'm doing to them, specially when you're in MR and going back to doing something in HR you missed and the poor thing just has no chance whatsoever. This issue hasnt manifested in any other MonHun game for me, including Rise, I wonder how i'll feel about it once I play Wilds.

Its important to consider that a lot of these issues WERE present in the base game, but the way I saw it was like who cares? The base game for me wasn't all that interesting, here however there is SO MUCH POTENTIAL! Change a handful of odd decisions and you easily get a generational MonHun entry that can truly be a Forever Game^tm.

All in all it's a shame for me to think of how good this game can be, because it'll always stand under the shadow of its own baffling design choices.

## Rating: 🌑🌑🌓🌕🌕
