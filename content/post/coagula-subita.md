## How would one compose silence? 

As someone who's often composing quite noisy material, both in the loudness and textural sense, I've always found myself intrigued by quiet pieces. Cage's 4'33" is an obvious example that comes to mind but I'm also refering to minimal ambient compositions, or whispered vocals, or synthesizers so shy they almost seem lonely.

This piece didn't necessarily start with this objective in mind. As all things in life tend to be it was a chaotic mix of random ideas that would prop up on a day and disappear just as easy the next. Samples taken from things that were breaking in my apartment, and in my university, and on museums. It wasn't until I started to lay out these sounds in my DAW that I heard it: The silence after listening to these often loud samples was mesmerizing! And it clicked.

I've felt this before, ever since my late teen years spending my free time alone in my room listening to silly little black metal records recommended to me on image boards online. The moment a dense record ends. Surely you too have felt it, that imposing silence after nearly an hour of carefuly crafted cachohonies. That silence that feels oh so special, for it allows your brain to process what its just experienced. That gentle silence that nowadays can easily be ruined by a streaming platform auto-playing some other record afterwards.

And so I got to work, this silence having become my new objective. Although I knew I could never reach it's full effect within a short composition I wanted to see what of it could be made seen.
